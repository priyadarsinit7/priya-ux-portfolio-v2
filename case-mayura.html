02 — Problem

Meaningful products, lost in a confusing experience.

Mayura Roots sells unique traditional Indian products — but users couldn't find them, understand them, or trust the platform enough to buy.

🗺️

Navigation is the core issue

Too many options, no clear categorization — users felt lost and gave up browsing.

📖

No cultural storytelling

Products had no origin, meaning, or usage context — users didn't know what they were buying or why it mattered.

🛡️

Trust & credibility gaps

Outdated design and sparse product info reduced confidence in authenticity and quality.

🧭

No guidance for first-timers

New buyers expected the site to guide them through unfamiliar products — like a knowledgeable shopkeeper would.

"Purposeful product seekers need a clear and trustworthy way to explore and purchase cultural products — because the current site lacks intuitive navigation, detailed product information, and cultural storytelling."

— Problem Statement

How Might We…

How might we redesign the product detail page to build trust and confidence for buyers?

How Might We…

Help users understand the cultural significance of products before purchasing?

How Might We…

Guide users like a knowledgeable shopkeeper in a physical cultural store?

07 — Final Design

Three focused improvements.

↕ scroll to view full screen
Navigation Clarity

Find products the way you think about them

Replaced the confusing generic nav with occasion-based and tradition-based categories — matching users' mental models from card sorting. Added a clear hierarchy so users always know where they are.

Navigation by: Weddings · Festivals · Daily Pooja · Gifts
Breadcrumbs on every page — no more getting lost
Search with filters: region, material, occasion, price

💡 Card sorting revealed users think by occasion ("I need something for a wedding") not by product category ("Brass items"). The new nav reflects this.

↕ scroll to view full screen
Cultural Storytelling

Products with meaning, not just price tags

Each product page now includes a dedicated cultural context section — origin, tradition, significance, and usage guidance. Designed so first-time buyers feel like they're being guided by a knowledgeable shopkeeper.

Cultural origin story on every product
Usage guidance — how and when to use the product
Region and tradition tags for discoverability
Reviews + "share with family" feature

💡 Every interviewed user said the same thing: "I want to know what this is before I buy it." Cultural context is not a nice-to-have — it's the core value proposition.

↕ scroll to view full screen
Trust & Credibility

A shopping experience that earns confidence

Modern visual design paired with authentic photography, transparent product sourcing, and clear secure checkout signals. Designed to reduce bounce and increase purchase commitment.

Authentic artisan photography standards
Secure ordering & payment trust badges
Cultural details panel — origin, tradition, region
Handcrafted & ethically sourced badges

💡 Outdated design and sparse product info was the #1 trust barrier. Real photography and cultural detail panels directly addressed this.

11 — Reflection

What this project taught me.

🏛️

IA is the invisible backbone

The most impactful changes weren't visual — they were structural. Reorganizing the navigation transformed the entire experience without changing a single pixel of visual design.

🌍

Culture is UX context

For Mayura Roots, cultural knowledge isn't supplementary content — it's the primary UX requirement. Users can't confidently buy what they don't understand.

🃏

Card sorting is underrated

Card sorting gave us direct data on user mental models — better than any assumption about "logical" categorization. I'd use it on every IA project going forward.

🔎

Trust is earned through detail

Authenticity — real product origins, real artisan stories, real usage context — is a stronger trust signal than any design flourish or visual polish.

🛒

Both archetypes matter

Designing for Purposeful Seekers alone would have created a transactional site. Designing for Meanderers too created a cultural destination — far more valuable for the brand.

🔮

What I'd explore next

Deeper testing with diaspora communities internationally, and a full-scale design system to ensure cultural consistency across the growing product catalogue.
