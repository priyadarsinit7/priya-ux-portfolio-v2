02 — The Problem

Coordination chaos, broken sign-up, zero trust.

Users struggled to coordinate group dances across schedules and time zones — and the platform made it worse with a confusing experience.

📝

Broken sign-up flow

Users expected instant account creation but got redirected to a contact form — with no clear path forward.

🗺️

Overwhelming navigation

No clear hierarchy — users couldn't find where to go or understand what the platform offered them.

👤

Unclear user roles

Dancers, Organizers, and Choreographers shared one confusing experience with zero differentiation.

💰

No pricing transparency

Hidden costs and no content previews killed trust before users even reached sign-up.

The platform added friction at every turn — unclear navigation, no pricing transparency, and a broken sign-up that sent users to a contact form. Result: low trust and high drop-off before onboarding even began.

08 — Final Design

Three focused design decisions.

Design Decision 1

Explore before you commit

Dancers receive link + code → access tutorials, track progress, use looping + speed control. Preview videos with mirroring + speed control.

  • Browse 120+ dance routines by occasion & style
  • Preview video with mirroring and speed control
  • See transparent pricing before sign-up commitment
Design Decision 2

Manage everything in one place

Organizers get a dedicated dashboard to invite dancers, assign routines, track progress, and communicate — replacing fragmented WhatsApp coordination with one streamlined tool.

  • Invite dancers via email or shareable link
  • Assign routines and track individual learning progress
  • Communicate and schedule within the platform
  • Browse 120+ dance routines by occasion & style
  • See transparent pricing before sign-up commitment
Design Decision 3

Apply, upload, and build your profile

Choreographers have their own dedicated onboarding pathway — a clear "Apply to be a Choreographer" CTA with a streamlined application and profile creation flow replacing the opaque contact form.

  • Dedicated application with clear expectations upfront
  • Upload routines with metadata and difficulty levels
  • Track how many groups are using your routines

11 — Reflection

What I learned building this.

🎭

Role-first design is underused

Treating role differentiation as a first-class design constraint unlocked the biggest usability gains. This should be the default for any multi-role product.

🛡️

Trust signals are a structural problem

Missing pricing and social proof aren't just copy issues — they're IA problems requiring intentional placement throughout the entire user journey.

⚡

Move fast, but anchor to data

Three weeks forced ruthless prioritization. Heuristic evaluation gave us a defensible backlog faster than any stakeholder assumption list.

🔗

The invite flow is often forgotten

Most Dancers entered via a forwarded link — not the homepage. Always map the full entry surface, not just the primary marketing page.

🌏

Cultural context matters deeply

South Asian weddings carry deep emotional stakes around performance and family. Future iterations should hold space for those cultural delight moments.

🔮

What I'd explore next

In-app rehearsal mode with sync playback, mobile-native experience, and a post-wedding reflection feature to close the emotional loop.
